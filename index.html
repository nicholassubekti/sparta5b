Holy tour to India

Tirtayatra

Embark on a sacred journey through India's most revered spiritual destinations with Tirtayatra. This holy pilgrimage allows devotees to visit ancient temples, sacred rivers, and blessed sites that have been centers of divine worship for thousands of years.

Our carefully curated pilgrimage routes combine traditional rituals, meditation sessions, and cultural immersion to create a transformative spiritual experience. Join us in this sacred journey that has been undertaken by countless devotees throughout the ages.

Day 1 - Arrival Delhi
  • Welcome upon arrival at Delhi International airport - After meet and assist services, transfer to Hotel.
  • Overnight stay at Hotel
Day 2 - Delhi
  • Breakfast at Hotel
  • Then transfer to Mathura (about 4 hour drive)
Day 2 - Mathura
  • Visit Shri Krishna Janmbhumi and Dwarakadheesh Temple
  • Lunch at a Hotel.
  • Then transfer to closely located Vrindavan

Mathura is the Birth place of Lord Krishna. For about 3000 Year it was the hub of culture and civilization. Taj Mahal built by Shah Jahan in 1630 for his queen Mumtaz Mahal to enshrine her mortal remains. This architectural marvel is a perfectly proportioned masterpiece fashioned from white marble that stands testimony to the still of 20,000 craftsmen brought together from Persia, Turkey, France and Italy and who took 17 year to complete this 'Love Poem in Marble'.

Shri Krishna Janmbhumi is the Birth Place of Lord Krishna.

Dwarkadhish Temple is a temple dedicated to Krishna, who is worshiped here by the name Dwarkadhish (Dvārakādhīśa), or 'King of Dwarka'.

Day 2 - Vrindavan
  • After visit in Vrindavan, transfer to Agra (1.5 hour drive).
Day 2 - Agra
  • Dinner and overnight stay at Hotel.
Day 3 - Agra
  • Breakfast at Hotel.
  • Then visit World famous and one of the Seven Wonders of the World - Taj Mahal.
  • After Taj Mahal visit Transfer back to Delhi (about 4 hour drive).
  • Lunch on the way.

Taj Mahal is closed on every Friday

Day 3 - Delhi
  • Arrive Delhi and then visit Akshardham Temple.
  • Check in at Hotel in evening.
  • Dinner and overnight stay at Hotel.

This temple is closed on every Monday.

Akshardham is a Hindu temple and spiritual-cultural campus in Delhi, India.

Day 3 - Delhi
  • Late Breakfast at hotel. After breakfast transfer to Haridwar (230 km - about 5 hours drive).
Day 3 - Delhi
  • On arrival check - in at hotel.
  • Lunch at hotel.
  • Also enjoy the evening Arti at Har Ki Paudi
  • Dinner and overnight stay at Hotel.

Har Ki Pauri - is considered to be the city’s focal point, where devotees assemble for a holy dip in the Ganges. Legend says that this is where a drop of nectar fell as a result of the manthan - churning of the oceans - Mythlogically it signifies the start of the world.

Day 5 - Haridwar
  • Breakfast at Hotel.
  • Then visit Mansa Devi Temple.
  • Later transfer to Rishikesh (just 01 hour drive). Visit Laxman Jhoola and Swarg Ashram. En route also visit Sapt Rishi Ashram.

Mansa Devi Mandir (Temple) : Mansa Devi Mandir, on top of a hill near the center of town is reached by cable car or by a road that gently rambles uphill, starting from the Railway Road. The views around the temple are the main attractions here

Lakshman Jhula is a suspension bridge across the river Ganges.

The ashram was built in the memory of Swami Vishudhanand, the saint better known as Kali Kamli Wala (the saint with a black blanket).

Sapt Rishi Ashram is a beautiful ashram complex in Haridwar.

Day 5 - Haridwar
  • Lunch at a Hotel / Restaurant in Rishikesh.
  • Evening return back to Haridwar.
Day 5 - Haridwar
  • Dinner and Overnight stay at Hotel.
Day 5 - Haridwar
  • Breakfast at Hotel.
  • Morning transfer to Kurukshetra (4.5 hours drive).
Day 5 - Haridwar
  • Lunch at Hotel in Kurukshetra.
  • Visit Jyotisar, Bhisma Kund, Sannihit Sarovar, etc.
  • continue drive to Delhi (about 04 hours drive). On arrival transfer to hotel.

The Purans tell stories of how Lord Brahma created the Universe from Kurukshetra. The legend of Shiva and Sati comes alive in the temples dedicated to the mother Goddess one sees here. The Mahabharata battle fought here, extols innumerable places of pilgrimage, not to forget Kurukshetra as the land of Bhagwad Gita. The Brahma Sarover is the best place to begin the pilgrimage. Belief is, that Lord Brahma conceptualized the site of the tank, giving it special sanctity.

Bhisma Kund is the place where it is believed that Pitamaha Bhishma lay watching the famous battle after Arjun, created a bed of arrows for him.

Jyotisar, on the bank of Jyotisar Sarovar wetland, is a Hindu pilgrimage site in the city of Kurukshetra in Haryana state of India.

Day 5 - Haridwar
  • Dinner at a Local Restaurant.
  • After Dinner at a nice Local Restaurant and then transfer to airport to board the onward flight with sweet memories of India Tirtayatra Tour.
